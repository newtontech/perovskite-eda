INTERPRETATION
--------------
Scientific Implications:

1. Log P Dominance
   - Hydrophobicity is the most significant predictor
   - Suggests interfacial compatibility is crucial
   - May relate to moisture resistance and stability

2. Molecular Flexibility
   - Rotatable bonds show significant positive correlation
   - Indicates conformational adaptability is beneficial
   - May enhance defect passivation

3. Size Effects
   - Molecular weight shows weak but significant correlation
   - Larger modulators may provide better coverage
   - However, non-significant in rank tests (suggests threshold effect)

4. Electronic Properties
   - H-bond acceptors contribute positively
   - Electron-donating capacity appears important
   - Consistent across all correlation methods

Limitations:

1. Weak Effect Sizes
   - All correlations are weak (r < 0.1)
   - Limited predictive power for individual descriptors
   - Suggests complex, non-linear relationships

2. Multicollinearity
   - High inter-correlations among descriptors
   - Difficult to isolate individual effects
   - Requires advanced modeling techniques

3. Descriptor Coverage
   - Only 6 basic physicochemical properties
   - Missing quantum chemical descriptors
   - Lacks 3D molecular information
